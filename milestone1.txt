3Ô∏è‚É£ Networking Foundations

A strong understanding of networking protocols is essential for interpreting traffic behavior.

TCP (Transmission Control Protocol)

Establishes a reliable connection before communication.

Uses a three-step handshake mechanism.

Ensures ordered and guaranteed delivery.

Common in web applications and enterprise systems.

Frequently targeted in many cyber-attacks.

UDP (User Datagram Protocol)

Does not establish a connection.

Faster but less reliable.

Often used in streaming, gaming, and VoIP.

Less commonly exploited compared to TCP.

Additional Network Protocols

HTTP / HTTPS

DNS

SSH

ICMP

Observational Insight

Analysis of the dataset indicates that most attack traffic is concentrated in TCP-based communications, while UDP and ICMP contain fewer attack instances.
